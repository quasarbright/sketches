Chladni figures

A plate clamped at its center and driven at a resonant frequency vibrates in a standing wave. Some lines never move — the nodal lines. Sand bounces off the moving regions and collects along these still lines, drawing the mode.

The pattern

For a square plate the displacement of a simple mode is a difference of two standing waves, and the figure is the set where it vanishes:

$$ \cos(n\pi x)\,\cos(m\pi y) \;-\; \cos(m\pi x)\,\cos(n\pi y) \;=\; 0 $$

The integers $m$ and $n$ count the half-waves along each axis. Raising them multiplies the crossings — higher notes make busier figures.
