Epicycles

Draw any closed curve and it gets approximated as a sum of rotating circles (epicycles) — the same trick 3blue1brown's video uses to explain the Fourier series.

the math

The drawn curve is resampled to $M$ evenly spaced points and treated as a complex signal $z_n = x_n + i y_n$. Its discrete Fourier transform

$$X_k = \frac{1}{M}\sum_{n=0}^{M-1} z_n \, e^{-2\pi i kn/M}$$

gives a coefficient for each integer frequency $k$. Reconstructing with only the largest-amplitude coefficients,

$$z(t) \approx \sum_{k} X_k \, e^{i k t}$$

is exactly a chain of circles: the $k$-th term has radius $|X_k|$ and spins at speed $k$, each one's center riding on the tip of the last. The epicycles slider controls how many terms $k$ are kept — more terms, closer to your original drawing.
